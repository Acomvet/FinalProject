What is the Drive?

The drive is usually the first shot taken on a hole. Golfers use a driver club to hit the ball as far as possible while still keeping it in play.

A successful drive can make the rest of the hole much easier by leaving a shorter approach shot into the green.

Driving Technique

Good driving starts with proper setup and balance. Golfers should maintain a stable stance, smooth tempo, and controlled swing path throughout the shot.

While distance is important, accuracy is often more valuable because keeping the ball in the fairway leads to better scoring opportunities.

Common Driving Mistakes

  • Swinging too hard and losing control
  • Poor grip positioning
  • Incorrect ball placement on the tee
  • Trying to hit every drive as far as possible

Practicing consistent mechanics is one of the best ways to improve driving performance over time.

Practice and Improvement

Many golfers improve their driving by practicing at driving ranges and focusing on accuracy before power.

Developing a repeatable swing and learning proper timing can help golfers hit straighter and longer drives.
