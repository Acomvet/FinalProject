What is an Approach Shot?

An approach shot is the shot played toward the green after the drive. The goal is to place the ball close to the hole and create an easier putt.

Approach shots often require precision and distance control rather than maximum power.

Choosing the Right Club

Golfers use different clubs depending on the distance to the green. Irons and wedges are the most common clubs used for approach shots.

Choosing the correct club can help golfers control both distance and ball trajectory more effectively.

Accuracy Over Power

Unlike driving, approach shots focus more on accuracy than distance. Landing the ball safely on the green is often more important than trying to hit the ball as far as possible.

Professional golfers spend countless hours practicing distance control to improve scoring opportunities.

Practicing Approach Shots

Many golfers improve their approach game by practicing with wedges and short irons from different distances.

Consistent practice helps golfers develop better touch, ball striking, and confidence when aiming for the green.
