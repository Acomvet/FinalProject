What is Golf?

Golf is a sport that combines precision, patience, and consistency. Players attempt to complete each hole in the fewest number of strokes possible using a variety of clubs designed for different situations.

Modern golf dates back to 15th century Scotland and has grown into one of the most popular sports in the world. From powerful drives to accurate putts, every shot plays an important role in scoring well.

A Brief History of Golf

The game of golf originated in Scotland where players would hit stones toward targets using sticks. Over time, golf evolved into a structured sport with official rules, professional tournaments, and famous championship courses.

Today, millions of people play golf recreationally and competitively around the world.

Common Golf Clubs and Distances

Distances vary depending on player skill and swing speed, but this table gives a general idea of which clubs are commonly used at different ranges.

Club Typical Distance Common Use
Driver 230 - 300 yards Tee shots on long holes
3 Wood 210 - 240 yards Long fairway shots
5 Iron 160 - 190 yards Approach shots
7 Iron 140 - 170 yards Controlled approach shots
Pitching Wedge 90 - 130 yards Short approach shots
Putter On the green Rolling the ball into the hole
